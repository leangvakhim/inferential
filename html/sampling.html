Population vs. Sample

In inferential statistics, we want to learn about a large group (the Population). Because it's too difficult or expensive to measure everyone, we select a smaller, manageable subset (the Sample) to represent them.

Real-World Scenario

Goal: Find the average cups of coffee consumed per week by adults in "Brew City".

Population: All 100,000 adults in the city. (Too many to survey!)
Sample: A selected group of 1,000 adults that we will actually ask.
